I am building a VR Bus Simulator on Three.js.

I started out with just a green cube, a red background, and a blue post for a reference frame.

At first, I struggled on how to implement the physics of the "bus" where if you keep turning right all the way, you just go in a circle.

After hours of searching and reading code on threejs.org, I figured out the logic thanks to a website called vr.ff.com, some of my "bus" physics was inspired from that.

When the physics was done, now I need a real bus instead of this green cube.

So I did hours of google searching, reading three.js official documents, chatgpt-ing, and so on.

After a while, I finally got a hang of importing and manipulating 3d models, I also picked up some use of Blender along the way (lifesaver!!)

There is still collision that I haven't implemented yet, I'm thinking of putting transparent materials around the pavements of the map and implement collision. Let me know what you think about this strategy, and if there is any better way I should approach this problem! Thanks all!
