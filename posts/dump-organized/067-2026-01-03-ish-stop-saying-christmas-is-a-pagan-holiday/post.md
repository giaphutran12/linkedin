Stop saying Christmas is a pagan holiday.

It’s historically inaccurate. And frankly, it’s lazy history.

If you dig into the source code of Christmas, everything points back to Christ.

Here is the truth about the 3 biggest "myths":

1. Santa Claus IS Real
He wasn't invented by a soda company.
He was St. Nicholas, the Bishop of Myra (modern-day Turkey) in the 4th century.
Born wealthy, he gave everything away to the poor.
Legend says he secretly tossed gold through a window to save a poor man's daughters from destitute slavery. Generosity. Justice. Faith.
That is the real Santa.
2. The Christmas Tree
"It's from Thor," they say. "It's pagan worship."

False.

St. Boniface, an 8th-century English missionary, actually chopped down the pagan "Thunder Oak" in Germany to stop human sacrifice.
When Thor didn't strike him dead, the people converted.
Boniface pointed to a small fir tree growing among the roots.
"This is the wood of peace," he said. "It points to Heaven."
The tree isn't a symbol of paganism.
It’s a trophy of Christianity’s victory over it.

3. The Date (December 25)
"Christians stole the date from Sol Invictus!"

Actually, the pagans stole it from us.

- 274 AD: Emperor Aurelian creates the "Sol Invictus" festival.
- 221 AD: Christian historian Sextus Julius Africanus had already connected Christ's conception to March 25.
Theology, not astronomy, determined the date.
If Jesus was conceived on March 25 (Annunciation), he was born 9 months later. December 25.


No matter how hard the world is trying to, you just cannot have Christmas without Christ.
And you cannot have Christmas without the Mass.

Don't let the world secularize the most profound moment in history. Go to Church. Feast. Celebrate the King.

Christ is born! Let us glorify Him!
