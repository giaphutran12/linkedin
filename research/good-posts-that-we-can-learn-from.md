I stopped showing up to my own team's dinners. On purpose.

When we started MLV, we called ourselves "faMLV."
Group dinners. Pool parties. Gym sessions together.
We were 4 people in a summer office and it was fucking awesome.

Now I run a team of 70 across 7 countries.
Most of it is virtual.

I still try to build "culture."
I suggest team hangouts. I hop on casual calls.
They show up. They engage.

But I started noticing something.

You can invite everyone to drinks, and they'll come.
But they won't enjoy it the same way.

So I'm learning to step back.

At scale, culture isn't about your presence.
It's about giving people the space to build their own.

Hand them the credit card. Let them plan the dinner.
You don't need to be there for it to matter.

The best teams don't need the founder at every hangout.
They need the founder to trust them enough to have one without them.

Stanley helped me untangle this one. Sometimes you need to talk it out before it makes sense.
